Vault Structure

How the _knowledge/ layer integrates with your PARA-organized Obsidian vault. Focused on the Meetings pilot.

Caveat: We haven't seen the actual vault yet. The structure below is our best understanding based on what Anant has described. Actual folder names, file conventions, and transcription tool output format will be confirmed by the vault-audit skill. The architecture adapts to whatever the audit reveals.

Design Principle: Respect What Exists

Your PARA structure stays exactly as-is. The system adds one new thing: a vault-level _knowledge/ folder for cross-cutting entity and concept pages (Karpathy's "wiki" layer). Your existing notes, folder organization, and file locations don't change. The underscore prefix keeps generated content visually separate from your own work.

The Meetings Pilot: Before and After

Before (Today)

  • Meeting notes are free-form, varying formats
  • Transcripts exist but aren't always linked from notes
  • Stakeholder files exist but have no links to meetings
  • No structured frontmatter (date, attendees, projects)
  • No way to ask "all meetings with Dr. Muller about CAMS"
  • No entity pages — knowledge re-discovered from scratch each query
  • Obsidian graph view shows sparse connections

After (Pilot Complete)

  • Meeting notes have standardized frontmatter (date, attendees, projects, meeting_type, transcript link)
  • Transcripts linked bidirectionally from meeting notes
  • Stakeholder files linked to meeting history (wiki-links)
  • Related meetings discovered and cross-linked automatically
  • Structured queries: "all regulatory meetings in Q1" via frontmatter grep
  • Entity pages in _knowledge/ accumulate discussion timelines
  • Obsidian graph view shows rich meeting↔stakeholder↔project web

Stakeholder ↔ Meeting Linking

Stakeholders are the first "entities" in the knowledge graph. They already exist as MD files in Resources/Stakeholders/. The process-meeting skill creates bidirectional links: meeting notes wiki-link to stakeholder files, and stakeholder files get a "Meeting History" section with wiki-links back to meetings. Obsidian's backlinks feature makes these navigable immediately.

In the Meeting Note

--- date: 2026-03-15 type: meeting meeting_type: regulatory attendees: - "[[dr-klaus-muller]]" - "[[dr-anna-weber]]" projects: - "[[FY27-CAMS-Alignment]]" transcript: "[[2026-03-15-transcript]]" tags: [fda, 510k, pre-submission] --- # 2026-03-15: FDA Pre-Submission Meeting ## Related Meetings - [[2026-02-28-fda-strategy]] — Prior 510(k) discussion - [[2026-01-10-competitor-review]] — Competitor regulatory approach

In the Stakeholder File

The _knowledge/ Layer

This is Karpathy's "wiki" — the compounding artifact. Entity pages accumulate knowledge from every meeting, every research session, every project. They grow richer over time without manual maintenance.

Entity Pages

Dedicated pages for specific things, organized into typed subdirectories: products/, people/, companies/, regulations/, studies/. Each aggregates everything your vault knows about that entity. Updated incrementally — each source adds, never replaces.

Concept Pages

Pages for abstract topics: Regulatory Pathways for AI Devices, Clinical Evidence Requirements. Synthesize knowledge across multiple sources. These are the "reference articles" that Karpathy's pattern builds over time.

Sources & Analysis

sources/ holds per-source ingestion records (meetings/, literature/, interviews/) — "what did the wiki learn from this source?" analysis/ stores filed query results, comparisons, and explorations. These create the "explorations add up" feedback loop.

Special Files

index.md: Master catalog (read first on every query). log.md: Chronological record of ingestion. SCHEMA.md: Templates and conventions. hot.md: Rolling ~500-word context cache of "what's happening now." These files anchor the entire knowledge graph.

Example Entity Page: Discussion Timeline

The key innovation for meetings: Entity pages accumulate a chronological discussion thread. Instead of searching for related meetings every time, the entity page IS the thread.

--- type: entity category: regulation aliases: [510(k), 510k, FDA 510(k) pathway] last_updated: 2026-04-06 source_count: 7 tags: [regulatory, FDA, 510k, medical-device, SaMD] --- # FDA 510(k) — CAMS ## Summary The 510(k) premarket notification is our chosen regulatory pathway for the CAMS AI colonoscopy device in the US market. ## Discussion Timeline - [[2026-01-10-competitor-review]] — Noted Competitor X got 510(k) in 6 months - [[2026-02-28-fda-strategy]] — Decided to pursue 510(k) over De Novo - [[2026-03-15-fda-pre-sub]] — Dr. Muller raised IRB timeline concern - [[2026-04-01-regulatory-sync]] — Updated: submission target Q3 2026 # Each processed meeting appends to this timeline automatically. # Over time: a complete narrative thread per topic. ## Key Facts - Predicate device: Competitor X's cleared device (K231234) - Target submission: Q3 FY27 - Clinical evidence: retrospective + small prospective study - PCCP may be required (per 2024 FDA guidance) ## Related - [[_knowledge/entities/product-cams|Product: CAMS]] - [[_knowledge/entities/eu-mdr|EU MDR]] (parallel pathway) - [[_knowledge/concepts/regulatory-pathways-ai-devices|Regulatory Pathways]]

Key Design Rules

Rule 1: Raw files are immutable The system never modifies your original files. Transcripts, PDFs, DOCX — all stay as they are. Only new files are created (knowledge pages) and existing user notes are enhanced (frontmatter, wiki-links).
Rule 2: _knowledge/ is cross-cutting Entity and concept pages aggregate knowledge from ALL sources across the entire vault. A single entity page might draw from meetings, project docs, research papers, and your own notes.
Rule 3: Entity pages only append Each new source adds information to existing entity pages. Never replaces. The Discussion Timeline section grows with every meeting. Source attribution traces every claim.
Rule 4: Archives are frozen When content moves to Archives, it's no longer processed. Entity pages retain historical references but mark them as "from archived source."
Rule 5: Provenance is traceable Every knowledge page cites specific source notes with Obsidian wiki-links. You can always trace a claim back to the original meeting, document, or session.
Rule 6: vault-audit discovers, doesn't assume We don't impose templates or conventions without first understanding what exists. The vault-audit skill maps the real structure, and all subsequent skills adapt to it.
